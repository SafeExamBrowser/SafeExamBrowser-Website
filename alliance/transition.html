Why do you terminate the successful SEB Consortium?

Due to changes in the Swiss VAT regulations, we could not handle anymore the consortium construction with a reasonable administrative overhead. Besides that the legal body of a consortium according to Swiss law did not fit very well to the international membership structure.

Why do you transfer the SEB Consortium to an Alliance?

We considered several other options like an association according to Swiss law or a foundation according to Swiss law. All options we looked into bring in administrative overhead and/or some new VAT related issues. So building an Alliance located at and operated solely by ETH Zurich seems to be the best compromise.

What are the main differences between SEB Consortium and SEB Alliance?

  • The SEB Alliance is located at and operated by ETH Zurich solely.
  • ETH Zurich leads the Alliance and offers different contribution levels to parties interested in the evolution of the SEB open source project.
  • ETH Zurich provides the infrastructure for the management and the software engineering of the SEB project.
  • ETH Zurich appoints an Alliance manager who will provide administrative support to the SEB Alliance, and ensure the day-to-day running of the SEB Alliance.
  • The SEB Alliance council meets once or twice a year as a sounding board of the SEB open source project. The council prioritises SEB development goals, too.

What are the choices for current members of the SEB Consortium towards the new SEB Alliance?

A. Proceed as a contributor to the SEB Alliance

B. End your financial support of the SEB open source project with the end of the SEB Consortium in December 2020 and grant the surplus of your membership fees to the SEB Alliance

C. End your financial support of the SEB open source project with the end of the SEB Consortium in December 2020 and get back parts of your membership fees

Obviously, we would recommend option A.

Why do you built up a surplus in SEB-Consortium?

We finance SEB developer positions with the membership fees. We need a surplus to balance fluctuations in the count of members over the time. Besides that, we have been in preparation to hire a further SEB developer in Q1/2020. This plans had to be postponed due to Corona crisis and we have to wait until we return to (new) normal operations at ETH Zurich.
